Putting the effort into making good quality silage pays off. Studies show that silage cut in best conditions will save money in the long run. Dry matter yeild is a very important factor, which in laymans terms, is the ammount of dry solids in the grass. About 70% dry matter is ideal for feed to dairy cows. If the DMY is too high the and value of the feed goes down with reagrds to proteins and nutrients, and if the DMY is too low, well then you have wet silage which demisninhes the value.

Wet silage is costly in more ways than one. It is a hidden cost. The cost of bringing the silage to teh yard, in that you are effectively transporting water and less actaul feed. You have to store this feed. More water in silage bales will result in more rot in silage which the cows will not eat. There is a cost per bale of silage, 3 by 1 tonne bales of DMY 40% has less quality feed than 2 870KG bale of 70% DM. That is 1200 KG of DM for the 3 bales compared to 1218kg of DM for the 2 dryer bales. Cost per bale is approx EUR18 to pay contractor.
